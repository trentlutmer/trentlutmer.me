Prescient Co. - UX Designer

Note: I have removed confidential information from this case study. Views reflected are mine alone.

Prescient Co. is a manufacturing and construction company. The Pre-Construction team uses a spreadsheet to derive a project cost. They enter model data into a series of calculators built around the Prescient system. In turn, this provides our users with an estimated cost for a building. If selected, Prescient will manufacture and build that project.

I worked on the initiative to reduce margin leakage and automating their experience. I had a team of three engineers and a business analyst to research, wireframe, and prototype pages.

Challenges —

Early user research showed us many various problems:

  • Blame it on the data
  • Change is a constant circus
  • Experience and knowledge is key
  • Wedded in their ways
  • Ineffective communication

Once identified, I focused efforts on collaboration and instilling trust in the data.

Solutions —

The interaction splits into different configurations based on the Pre-Construction team's workflow. From setting the location to the part quantity, users can adjust their project.

Having the data imported into Phoenix, allows the user to focus on the result, as opposed to manual input. This idea went through many tests to find issues with navigation, configuration, and summary.

Feedback —

The test concluded in a single day and we were able to put several assumptions to rest. Some of the tasks could have been clearer and the prototype could be more flexible, but we achieved our goal. All participants found the application clean and organized. Yet users felt the navigation's ‘back and forth’ nature was confusing. They wanted a sense of gratification and received nothing. While the test did not yield the results we hoped, the new data and feedback will direct us. Most of the changes are simple, so we can iterate.
